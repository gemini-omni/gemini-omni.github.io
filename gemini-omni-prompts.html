How to Write Better Gemini Omni Prompts

A better prompt answers six questions: what is the subject, what is happening, where does it happen, how should the camera behave, what visual style should guide the output, and what channel or format is the final video for? Those six questions keep the scene legible. They also reduce the common problem of prompt drift, where the model starts with the right idea but loses the structure halfway through the clip.

For Gemini Omni text to video prompts, start with the scene and the camera. For Gemini Omni image to video prompts, anchor the movement and the details that must remain stable. Keep prompts detailed, but not chaotic. Too little detail creates ambiguity. Too much contradictory detail creates confusion. The strongest prompt feels like a focused creative brief.

Gemini Omni Prompts FAQ

What are the best Gemini Omni prompts?

The best prompts clearly define subject, action, camera, style, lighting, and output goal.

Can prompts work for image-to-video?

Yes. Image-to-video prompts should describe motion, framing, and which visual elements must stay consistent.

Should prompts be long?

Prompts should be detailed enough to guide the scene, but not overloaded with conflicting instructions.

How can I improve weak prompts?

Add scene context, action verbs, camera direction, style cues, and a clear output use case.
